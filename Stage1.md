# 2. Research and Brainstorming

During the brainstorming stage, the team explored multiple project ideas to identify a real-world problem that could be addressed through a digital platform.

Both individual research and group discussions were conducted to generate potential ideas. The team used several brainstorming techniques including:

**Mind Mapping**  
Mind maps were used to visually organize ideas and identify connections between problems and possible solutions.

**SCAMPER Framework**  
The SCAMPER method was used to analyze existing ideas and explore possible improvements or adaptations.

**How Might We Questions**

Examples of questions used during brainstorming included:

- How might we help people find reliable service providers?
- How might we simplify access to technical services?
- How might we organize service providers in a centralized system?

Through this process, several project ideas were generated and evaluated.

---

# 3. Ideas Explored

## Idea 1: University Student Hub Platform

### Description

A digital platform designed to support university students by providing access to academic resources such as study summaries, course materials, and schedule organization tools.

### Strengths

- Provides useful academic tools
- Encourages collaboration between students
- Helps students organize their academic tasks

### Weaknesses

- Limited target audience
- Similar platforms already exist
- Requires continuous content updates
- Content quality depends on student contributions

### Reason for Rejection

This idea was rejected because the platform targets a narrow audience and several similar solutions already exist. Additionally, maintaining updated academic content would require continuous effort.

---

## Idea 2: Training Coordination Platform for Non-Profit Organizations

### Description

A platform connecting non-profit organizations with professional trainers. Organizations could search for trainers to conduct workshops, while trainers could present their expertise and available programs.

### Strengths

- Supports educational initiatives
- Creates networking opportunities
- Helps organizations find qualified trainers

### Weaknesses

- Requires partnerships with organizations
- Difficult to verify trainer qualifications
- Platform success depends on external participation
- Requires scheduling coordination

### Reason for Rejection

The idea was rejected because it relies heavily on external partnerships that would be difficult to establish within the available project timeline.

---

## Idea 3: Ammar Platform (Selected Idea)

### Description

Ammar is a digital platform that connects users with construction companies, engineering consultants, and technical workers within a specific city.

The platform helps users easily find reliable service providers, request services or appointments, and manage service interactions in one centralized system.

# 5. Selected MVP Concept

## Ammar Platform

The selected MVP is **Ammar**, a city-focused platform that simplifies access to construction and technical services.

The platform enables users to find service providers such as construction companies and technicians, request appointments, and manage service interactions through a structured digital system.

---

# 6. Problem Statement

Finding reliable construction companies or technical workers can be challenging. Service providers are often discovered through scattered sources such as social media, personal recommendations, or informal contacts.

This fragmented process makes it difficult for users to compare providers, evaluate reliability, and manage service requests efficiently.

---

# 7. Proposed Solution

Ammar provides a centralized platform where users can:

- Discover construction companies and technicians
- View provider profiles and ratings
- Request service appointments
- Track service request status
- Receive reports or documents after consultations
- Leave reviews and ratings

The platform also allows providers to manage incoming service requests through dedicated dashboards.

---

# 8. Target Users

The platform targets individuals who require engineering or technical services within the city, including:

- Homeowners
- People building new houses
- Individuals renovating their homes
- Users needing maintenance services

---

# 9. System User Types

The platform supports three types of users.

## Users (Customers)

Customers can:

- Browse companies and technicians
- Request services or appointments
- Track request status
- View consultation reports and documents
- Rate and review service providers

## Companies

Construction or engineering consulting companies can:

- Create company profiles
- Receive appointment requests
- Accept or reschedule appointments
- Upload consultation reports or related files

## Technicians (Workers)

Technicians provide maintenance services such as electrical, plumbing, or repair work. They can:

- Create technician profiles
- Receive service requests
- Accept or reject requests
- Complete services and receive ratings

---

# 10. Key Features (MVP)

To ensure feasibility within the project timeframe, the Minimum Viable Product will include the following features:

- User registration and authentication
- Browsing service categories
- Viewing provider profiles
- Requesting service appointments
- Appointment confirmation or rescheduling
- Request status tracking
- User rating and review system
- Post-service reports and file attachments
- Simple gamification through provider badges such as:
  - Top Rated Technician
  - Trusted Company

---

# 11. Potential Challenges

During brainstorming and evaluation, several potential challenges were identified:

- Collecting accurate provider information
- Ensuring fairness in the rating system
- Managing appointment scheduling
- Maintaining a simple and intuitive user experience

---

# 12. Opportunities

Despite these challenges, the platform also presents opportunities for future expansion:

- Expanding the platform to multiple cities
- Adding more service categories
- Integrating location-based services
- Enhancing provider management tools

---

# 13. Project Scope

To maintain a realistic and manageable development process, the platform will initially focus on **a single city**.

This approach reduces system complexity and allows the team to deliver a functional MVP within the available timeframe.

Future versions of the platform may expand to additional cities and services.

---

# 14. Decision-Making Process

The final project idea was selected through a structured evaluation process. The team discussed each proposed idea, analyzed its advantages and disadvantages, and evaluated feasibility based on available resources and development time.

After careful comparison, the team concluded that the Ammar platform provides the most practical and impactful solution while remaining achievable within the project constraints.
